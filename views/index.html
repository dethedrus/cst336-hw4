Virii Throughout the Ages

The theory of bad faith computer code is nearly as old as the modern computer, exemplified by John von Neumann's essay, Theory of Self-Reproducing Automata (1966).

Another very early example of the concept was visualized by astophysicist and fiction author Gregory Benford in his novel The Scarred Man (1970)

Even during the early age of the ARPANET, Bob Thomas (researcher at BBN Technologies) created a self-replicating program called Creeper, which was intended to infect DEC PDP-10 computers. Thankfully it was a proof of concept only intended to push the message "I'm the creeper, catch me if you can!" Ray Tomlinson later created a cleanup program called Reaper to remove it, though it is still technically a self-replicating worm as with Creeper.
